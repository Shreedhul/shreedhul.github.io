A Neuroscientific Approach to a Modifed Version of MBTI

MBTI is a classification of personalities that is generally regarded in the scientific community to be incorrect. It categorises people into 16 different personality types. A large criticism is that these 16 categories are very binary, and can not account for the nuance and continuous aspects of personality, and is considered pop-psychology. MBTI is an extension from, though independent of the theories of psychologist Dr Jung, who, from my understanding, are not the mainstream in science, though I believe they are popular. I believe that due to simplifications made for ease of consumption to increase reach, as well as what I believe to be incompleteness of the theory, MBTI has a bad name in the scientific community, but is actually largely a brilliant and correct model. One of my goals is to explain it via structures of the brain. Again, this is not a scientific journal, just throwing around ideas!
