The Current & Future Landscape of AI

March - April 2026

The hypotheticals shrouding AI are drastic. As another technological revolution speeds down the highway, even the brightest of humanity’s headlights don’t pierce far through the misty future. Somewhere deep in the frightening blackness is our fate, yet the answer to what lays beyond is locked in a safe as time selfishly hoards the key.

Currently I am working on a project spanning several blogs to sketch out the current landscape of AI (as of March 2026) and from my view point of course!

These have been linked in the order they are "supposed" to be read in, but it's a free world and nothing is stopping you from reading out of order!



The Current & Future Landscape of AI: Technology Behind AI

Here I will be outlining the basics of the technology of LLMs, a brief history of the technology and the possibilities of the future of the technology can go. I also go into who this affects, and how. This will be non-technical, and go over what the terms AI and LLM actually means, the realisation of the size of training datasets, the birth of the Transformer architecture, the rapid improvement of AI in the past few years, and the diminishing rate of improvement we are starting to see.



The Current & Future Landscape of AI: Economy

The economics of AI has two sides. The economics of these companies creating and training their LLMs, and the economics of the average person. Many anticipate to be financially ragdolled as LLM reasoning is expected to grow in adoption and consequently play a much larger role in the various processes and jobs needed in the upkeep of society. Is this fear justified? Both sides will be explained in this blog. I will go over how non of these large companies are profitable, how they plan to fix that, the astronomical valuation of these companies, the fears of an AI bubble, as well as what is expected to happen to the average person.



The Current & Future Landscape of AI: Environment

It's no secret that the training of LLMs has a negative impact on the environment. I'll be going over why exactly that is, why people using LLMs isn't as impactful for the environment as some may think, how worried we should be for its future impact on the environment, and the effects data centers have on the local communities where they are built.



The Current & Future Landscape of AI: Technology Using AI

Here I will be listing what AI has been used for in the past, what it is currently being used for with it's widespread and what we speculate it will be used for in the future to try and paint a picture of an AI powered future!



The Current & Future Landscape of AI: Law, Regulation & Alignment

Fears of robots taking over the world has been present in sci-fi long before the AI boom. Now, people fear humanity is now genuinely at threat, and even influential leaders in the AI space are cautious of the dangers this technology could cause. AI alignment, which is getting these LLMs to be aligned with humans in their goals, is essential for the saftey of mankind and for getting the most out of AI that we can. Outside alignment, AI has the potential to cause harm in several ways, and there have already been several of such instances. Law and regulation is the answer, but they haven't caught up yet. I will cover all these topics in this blog.



The Current & Future Landscape of AI: Geo-Politics

West vs East, US vs China. The big race to AGI. What is AGI and why are we racing to it? Who is "winning"? Who is predicted to "win"? What's the prize for "winning"? More generally, how is AI development and adoption spreading around the world, and how does adopting AI impact any given country? What is the gain in other countries adopting your country's model? I'll answer all these questions in this blog.



The Current & Future Landscape of AI: Public Perceptions and Thinking

There are few problems as polarising as AI. It's no surprise given how wide ranging and quickly it's adoption is and is expected to be. There are lots of big questions, such as: Are we headed for extinction? What is the mental impact of relying on AI, especially growing up? Are people relying more and more on socialising, and will society lose its social skills? What are it's effects on mental health? What about the issues that can arise from AI getting things wrong? And many more. The politics of AI is huge. Also, how positively people feel about the development of AI is an extremely mixed bag, with a lot feeling extremely negative about it. Why is that, and is that a fair reaction to AI? I will be covering these topics in this blog.
